## Project description

Autaptic excitatory neuron can produce enhanced sensitivity to input patterns. This may serve as a neuromodulateable mechanism
of attentional control, sensory gating, or winner-takes-all dynamics. See google docs.

Several of these mechanisms seem to rely on a few mechanisms:
    
- spike entrainment (coherence) - can one neuron can force the other to follow its firing rhythm?
- competing inhibition - can one neuron force another population to be inhibited?
- Certain frequencies are associated with task-decision and information gating. e.g. gamma oscillations within theta oscillations, the theta oscillation provide the gating window, and information if processed at the gamma frequency.

### Model Networks
#### 2 excitatory neurons
    2 excitatory neurons with different inputs. Can you tune the autapse to make the non-autaptic neuron follow the autaptic neuron?
    Suggested as the main means of information transfer.
#### 1 excitatory, 1 inhibitory neuron
    Can the excitatory neuron direct the inhibitory neuron to inhibit as a selected frequency (presumably, if the above is true).
    
    Can you tune the inhibitory autapse to determine the firing rate of the excitatory neuron?

#### 3 neurons: 2 excitatory, 1 inhibitory
    Putting these 2 smaller systems together, can you force synchrony between the excitatory neurons at a frequency determined by the inhibitory interneuron's autapse?
